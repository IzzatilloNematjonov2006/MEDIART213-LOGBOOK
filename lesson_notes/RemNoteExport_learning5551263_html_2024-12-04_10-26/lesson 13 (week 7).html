  • Internet is servers located all around the world
  • Cookies contain Metadata
  • user's browsing habits, preferences, and website activities
  • data characterizes web habit to improve experience
    • Enhanced Security by Authentication and Fraud Prevention
    • Improved Website Functionality by Tracking User Behavior, Traffic and Performance Analysis
  • Types of Cookies:
  • Session Cookies [held the fluidity of your web activity]
  • Permanent Cookies [opposite of Session Cookies. They have life span]
  • Secure Cookies [can be transmitted only via secure networks]
  • Supercookie (not that bad honestly) [Internet supplier using. Some hackers may also use, if they can]
  • Zombie Cookies (bad) [You think you cleaned, but it actually stays in your device]
  • Third-Party Cookies (really bad)
  • There are regulations to regulate companies' use of cookies:
  • General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)- European Union
  • California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) - United States
  • Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) - United States
